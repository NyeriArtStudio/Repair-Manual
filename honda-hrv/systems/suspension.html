Suspension & Steering

Front: independent MacPherson strut with lower control arms and stabiliser bar. Rear: torsion beam axle with coil springs and separate shocks. Electric power-assisted rack and pinion steering (EPAS).

Front Suspension

ComponentSpecification
TypeMacPherson strut with L-shaped lower arm
Spring free length368 mm (approx - check spec for your model)
Stabiliser bar diameter23 mm (varies by model)
Wheel bearing typeDouble-row angular contact ball bearing (unit)
Wheel bearing torque250 Nm (new), 200 Nm (reuse)

Note: Front wheel bearings are sealed units pressed into knuckle. Not adjustable, not serviceable. Replace when play or noise detected.

Rear Suspension

ComponentSpecification
TypeTorsion beam with trailing arms
Spring free length320 mm (approx)
Shock absorber typeGas-filled twin-tube
4WD rear differentialMounted on torsion beam

Steering

ParameterValue
TypeRack and pinion with EPAS
Power assist typeElectric (motor on column)
FluidNone (electric system)
Steering wheel free play0-10 mm measured at rim

EPAS note: Electric power steering has no fluid to check. If heavy steering: check battery voltage (low voltage reduces assist), check EPAS fuse, check for DTCs in EPAS module.

Wheel Alignment

ParameterValue
Toe (front)0 +/- 2 mm (total toe)
Camber (front)-0°30' +/- 45'
Caster2°35' +/- 45'
King pin inclination13°10' +/- 1°
Toe (rear)2 +/- 2 mm (in, total)
Camber (rear)-1°05' +/- 45'

Only front toe is adjustable. If camber or caster is out, inspect for damaged components. Rear toe is non-adjustable - bent trailing arm or torsion beam will require replacement.

Source: Honda HR-V Shop Manual, Chapters 18 (Suspension) and 17 (Steering).
