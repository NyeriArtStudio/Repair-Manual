Cooling System

Pressurised cooling system with aluminium radiator, belt-driven water pump, and dual electric cooling fans. Uses Honda Type 2 coolant (blue) or equivalent ethylene glycol long-life antifreeze.

Specifications

ParameterValue
System capacity5.0 litres
Coolant typeHonda Type 2 (blue) or equivalent long-life ethylene glycol
Antifreeze ratio50/50 minimum (do not exceed 70/30)
Radiator cap pressure88-108 kPa (0.9-1.1 kgf/cm²)
Fan switch-on temp90-95°C (low speed), 100-105°C (high speed)
ECT sensor resistance (at 80°C)250-400 ohms (approx)
Coolant Warning

Always use Honda Type 2 coolant or equivalent long-life ethylene glycol. Do not mix different coolant types (green, red, blue have different corrosion inhibitors). If system is drained, refill with correct type and bleed thoroughly. Air pockets cause overheating and heater failure.

Radiator

Aluminium core with plastic tanks. Automatic transmission models have integral CVT fluid cooler in radiator tank. Check for external fin damage and internal coolant contamination (oil in coolant indicates AT cooler failure).

Replacement Procedure

1

Drain coolant via radiator drain plug.

2

Disconnect upper and lower hoses, reservoir hose.

3

CVT models: disconnect CVT fluid cooler lines (plug to prevent leakage).

4

Disconnect cooling fan connectors. Remove mounting bolts. Lift radiator with fans attached upward.

Thermostat

TypeWax pellet, with jiggle valve
Opening temperature80-84°C
Fully open95°C at 8.0 mm lift minimum
LocationHousing on front of cylinder head

Jiggle valve position: Must face upward (12 o'clock) when installing. This allows air bleeding during filling.

Water Pump

Belt-driven from crankshaft. Common failure: bearing noise, seal leak (weep hole shows coolant), impeller corrosion (reduced flow).

Timing belt drives the water pump. Always replace water pump when changing timing belt. Pump bolts: 12 Nm.

Cooling Fans

Dual electric fans behind radiator. Two-speed operation controlled by thermo switch and ECM.

ConditionFan Operation
Below 90°CFans off
90-95°CLow speed (series connection)
100-105°CHigh speed (parallel connection)
AC onFans run continuously (at least low speed)

Fan Motor Test

1

Disconnect fan motor connector. Apply 12V directly to motor terminals.

2

Motor should run smoothly. Current draw: 4-8A (low speed), 8-15A (high speed).

3

If motor runs but not via normal control: check fan relay, thermo switch, and wiring.

Cooling System Testing

System Pressure Test

1

Attach pressure tester to radiator neck in place of cap.

2

Pressurise to 108 kPa (1.1 kgf/cm²). Hold 2 minutes. Should not drop more than 14 kPa.

3

If pressure drops with no external leak: suspect head gasket (check for combustion gases in coolant with tester).

ECT Sensor Test

Disconnect ECT sensor connector. Measure resistance across sensor terminals:

At 0°C4.5-6.5 kΩ
At 20°C2.0-3.0 kΩ
At 40°C0.9-1.3 kΩ
At 80°C250-400 Ω

Coolant Refill & Bleeding

1

Close drain plug. Fill radiator to neck. Fill reservoir to MAX.

2

Start engine. Run at 2000 rpm with heater on HOT and fan on HIGH. This opens thermostat and circulates coolant through heater.

3

As thermostat opens, coolant level drops. Top up as needed.

4

When upper hose is hot and bubbles stop, install cap. Let cool, recheck reservoir level.

Cooling Quick Diagnosis

Overheating: Check coolant level, thermostat (remove and test), fan operation (jumper test), water pump impeller, radiator blockage.
No heat from heater: Low coolant level, air lock in heater circuit, blocked heater matrix, stuck heater valve.
Coolant loss no external leak: Head gasket (check for oil in coolant, white smoke, combustion gas test).
