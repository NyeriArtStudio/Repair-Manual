Fuel & Emissions System

Honda PGM-FI (Programmed Fuel Injection) is a multi-point electronic fuel injection system. Uses sequential port injection with one injector per cylinder. Managed by ECM/PCM (Engine/Powertrain Control Module).

PGM-FI System Specifications

ParameterValue
System typeMulti-point sequential EFI
Fuel pressure (idle)260-310 kPa (38-45 psi)
Fuel pressure (vacuum hose off)320-370 kPa (46-54 psi)
Injector resistance10-14 ohms at 20°C

Fuel Pump & Relay

Fuel Pump

Location: Inside fuel tank, accessed via panel under rear seat (left side).
Type: Electric, turbine-type, immersed in fuel for cooling.
Fuel pressure: 260-310 kPa at idle.
Electrical: 12V, fused via PGM-FI main relay.

Fuel Pump Circuit Note

The fuel pump is controlled by the PGM-FI main relay. When ignition is turned ON, the relay runs the pump for 2 seconds to prime, then shuts off until engine is cranking/running. This is a common Honda failure point - if the relay contacts are worn, the pump won't prime or run.

Pressure test: Connect fuel pressure gauge to service port on fuel rail. Turn ignition ON (do not start). Pressure should reach 260+ kPa within 2 seconds. At idle: 260-310 kPa. With vacuum hose removed from pressure regulator: 320-370 kPa. If pressure is low: suspect pump, filter, or regulator.

OEM pump: Honda 17040-S2H-A01
Aftermarket: Bosch 0 580 313 159, Walbro GCA3350
Filter: Honda 16010-S2H-A01 (integral with pump assembly)

PGM-FI Main Relay

Location: Under-dash fuse/relay box, left side.
Function: Supplies power to fuel pump, injectors, and ECM.
Common failure: Solder joints crack on relay PCB. Symptoms: no fuel pump prime, no start, engine stalls intermittently.
Fix: Resolder all joints on relay board, or replace relay.
OEM: Honda 39400-S2H-003 (verify against your VIN)

O-ring colour code: New injectors come with colour-coded O-rings. Always use new O-rings and lubricate with clean engine oil before installation.

Throttle Body

Contains throttle valve, Idle Air Control (IAC) valve, and Throttle Position Sensor (TPS). Idle speed is controlled by ECM via IAC valve - not adjustable mechanically.

1

Remove air intake ducting. Clean throttle bore and plate with throttle body cleaner (not carb cleaner - may damage coatings).

2

Check TPS voltage: should be 0.5V at idle, increasing smoothly to 4.5V at WOT.

3

Check IAC valve resistance: 10-14 ohms. Remove and clean plunger/seat if idle is erratic.

EVAP System

Evaporative emission control captures fuel vapours from tank and stores in charcoal canister. Purged to engine under ECM control.

Two-Way Valve Test

1

Remove valve from canister. Blow into tank side port: should allow air flow at 0.5-2.5 kPa.

2

Blow into canister side port: should allow air flow at 5.9-12.7 kPa.

3

If valve doesn't open at specified pressures, or leaks in both directions, replace.

Honda PGM-FI Quick Diagnosis

No start: Listen for 2-second fuel pump prime when ignition turned ON. If no prime, check PGM-FI main relay and fuse. If pump runs but no start, check fuel pressure at rail.
Hard start hot: Check fuel pressure holding after shutdown (should stay above 150 kPa for 5 min). If pressure drops fast, suspect leaking injector or fuel pressure regulator.
Idle hunt/erratic: Clean IAC valve and throttle body. Check for vacuum leaks. Check TPS voltage sweep is smooth.
