Braking System

Disc brakes all round. Front: ventilated discs with single-piston floating calipers. Rear: solid discs with integrated drum handbrake. Vacuum servo-assisted. ABS standard on all models.

Front Disc Brakes

Single-piston sliding caliper. Piston is exposed - can be pushed back for pad replacement without removing caliper.

Pad Replacement

1

Remove lower caliper pin bolt. Pivot caliper up.

2

Remove old pads and shims. Clean caliper bracket.

3

Push piston back into caliper bore using old pad and G-clamp. Check brake fluid reservoir doesn't overflow.

4

Fit new pads and shims. Apply anti-squeal compound to back of pads (not friction surface).

5

Pivot caliper down. Torque pin bolt: 30-35 Nm.

Rear Discs & Handbrake

Rear caliper has a screw-type piston (not push-back type). Piston must be rotated clockwise while applying pressure to retract (for pad clearance over handbrake drum).

Tool required: Rear caliper wind-back tool, or needle-nose pliers in piston notches.

Handbrake Shoe Adjustment

Self-adjusting via service brake operation. If handbrake travel is excessive: remove rear caliper and disc. Check shoe condition. Adjust via star wheel until wheel just locks, then back off 8 clicks.

ABS System

2-channel ABS controlling front wheels independently and rear wheels together (via proportioning valve). ABS unit located on left side of engine compartment.

ComponentLocation
ABS modulatorLeft engine bay, brake lines connected
ABS ECUIntegrated with modulator or separate under dash
Front wheel speed sensorsFront hubs (integrated)
Rear wheel speed sensorsRear hubs (integrated)

ABS diagnosis: Jump SCS connector. Count ABS warning light flashes. Common codes: 1-4 = individual wheel sensors, 5 = pump motor, 6 = solenoid valve.

Hydraulic System

Dual diagonal circuits. Master cylinder directly mounted to servo. Fluid reservoir has MIN/MAX marks. Fluid should be crystal clear - dark fluid indicates moisture contamination.

Bleeding Procedure

1

Fill reservoir with fresh DOT 3/4 fluid. Attach bleed hose to nipple (10 mm wrench).

2

Order: RR, LR, RF, LF.

3

Assistant pumps pedal 3 times, holds. Open nipple 1/2 turn. Close before pedal hits floor.

4

Repeat until clear fluid, no bubbles. Check reservoir level between wheels.

Source: Honda HR-V Shop Manual, Chapter 19 (ABS section reference).
