Honda HR-V

1999 - 2006 (GH series) - 3-door and 5-door models

Vehicle Overview

The Honda HR-V (Hi-rider Revolutionary Vehicle) was Honda's compact crossover SUV. Built on a modified Civic platform with a raised ride height and optional Real Time 4WD system. Two body styles: 3-door (early) and 5-door (added later). All engines use Honda's PGM-FI fuel injection and VTEC variable valve timing.

VTEC Note (D16W5)

The D16W5 VTEC system switches cam profiles at approximately 4800 rpm. A solenoid on the cylinder head controls oil pressure to the VTEC rocker arms. If VTEC is not engaging, check oil level first (low oil pressure prevents operation), then test the VTEC solenoid valve. The system requires clean oil - follow Honda's 6-month/10,000 km oil change interval strictly.

Timing Belt Critical Warning

The D16W1/D16W5 is an interference engine. Timing belt failure will cause piston-to-valve contact and catastrophic engine damage. Honda specify replacement every 80,000 km or 48 months - whichever comes first. Always replace the tensioner and water pump at the same time. Do not exceed this interval.
