Suspension & Steering

Front: independent MacPherson strut with coil springs, lower wishbones, and anti-roll bar. Rear: non-independent torsion beam axle with trailing arms and separate telescopic shock absorbers. Rack and pinion steering with optional power assistance.

Front Suspension

ComponentSpecification
TypeMacPherson strut with lower wishbone
Spring typeCoil spring around damper
Anti-roll barYes, connected to lower wishbones
Wheel bearing typeTaper roller (adjustable preload)
Wheel bearing preload10 Nm while rotating, then back off 90°

Strut Replacement Notes

Strut insert (damper cartridge) can be replaced without removing complete strut from vehicle. Spring compressor required. Always renew both sides together.

OEM dampers: Boge/Sachs, Bilstein (sport option)
Spring free length (new): Approx 370 mm (varies by engine weight)

Balljoint Check

1

Jack up front and support on axle stands. Grasp wheel at 12 and 6 o'clock positions.

2

Rock wheel. Any movement indicates balljoint wear or wheel bearing play.

3

Place lever under tyre and lift. Watch lower balljoint. Any vertical movement = balljoint worn.

4

Check balljoint dust cover for splits. If damaged, renew balljoint (grease will be contaminated).

Rear Suspension

ComponentSpecification
TypeTorsion beam axle with trailing arms
Spring typeCoil springs (some early 1.05 had torsion bars)
Damper typeTelescopic, separate from springs
Beam mountingRubber bushings to body
Wheel bearingsTaper roller, adjustable

Rear Axle Beam Bushings

Common wear point. Symptoms: clunking over bumps, wandering rear end, tyre wear. Bushings are pressed into beam and not separately available on early models - complete beam may be needed. Aftermarket polyurethane bushings available.

Steering

SpecificationValue
TypeRack and pinion
Power steering typeHydraulic (ZF or TRW)
Power steering fluid (pre-April 1989)Dexron II ATF
Power steering fluid (post-April 1989)VW G 002 000 mineral oil
Pump belt deflection10 mm under firm finger pressure
Steering wheel free playMax 25 mm measured at rim
Power Steering Fluid Warning

Pre-1989 and post-1989 systems use incompatible fluids. Dexron ATF in post-1989 system will destroy seals. Check reservoir cap marking: "ATF only" or "VW G 002 000 only". Never mix fluids.

Wheel Alignment

ParameterValue
Toe (total)0 +/- 10 minutes (parallel to slight toe-in)
Camber (front)-30' +/- 30' (approx -0.5°)
CasterNot adjustable (check for bent components)
King pin inclinationNot adjustable

Note: Only toe is adjustable via track rod ends. If camber or caster is out of spec, check for bent suspension components or accident damage.

Testing Procedures

Shock Absorber Test

Bounce each corner of vehicle. Should return to rest position after 1-2 oscillations. If continues bouncing, damper is worn. Also inspect for oil leakage (mist of oil is normal weeping, dripping is failure).

Steering Rack Check

With engine running, turn steering lock to lock. Should be smooth with no notchiness. Check rack gaiters for splits (common MOT failure). If power steering: check fluid level and belt tension.

Source: Haynes Manual 1081, Chapter 10.
