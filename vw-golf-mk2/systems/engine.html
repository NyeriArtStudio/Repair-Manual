Engine Mechanical

All VW Golf MK2 engines are four-cylinder, in-line, water-cooled, overhead camshaft units mounted transversely at the front. The camshaft is driven by a toothed belt which also drives the coolant pump. This section covers all engines from 1.05L to 1.8L 16V.

1.05 & 1.3 Litre Pre-August 1985

Rocker-finger type valve gear with adjustable clearances. The distributor is driven directly from the camshaft.

1.05 & 1.3 Litre Post-August 1985

Hydraulic tappets (bucket type) - self-adjusting, no routine valve clearance check required. Maximum permissible hydraulic tappet free travel: 0.1 mm.

All other specifications identical to pre-August 1985 models except valve clearances are not adjustable.

Hydraulic Tappet Note

If valve train noise is excessive, check oil pressure first. Low oil pressure (below 2.0 bar at 2000 rpm) will cause tappet noise. Do not use engine flush products - these can clog the small oil passages in the tappets.

Key Components

Timing Belt & Tensioner

[Image: Timing belt layout showing crankshaft sprocket, camshaft sprocket, coolant pump, and tensioner roller]

Tool: VW 210/220 tensioner pins

The toothed timing belt drives the camshaft and coolant pump. Tension is set by rotating the coolant pump on its eccentric mount (pre-1985) or via an automatic tensioner (later models).

Replacement interval: Every 40,000 miles (60,000 km) - CRITICAL. Always renew idler roller and tensioner at the same time. Belt failure = catastrophic engine damage (interference design).

OEM: VW 026 109 119 / INA 531 0145 10
Aftermarket: Dayco 94160, Gates 5050, Contitech CT637

Camshaft

Single overhead camshaft on 8V engines, DOHC on 16V. Driven by toothed belt. Endfloat limit: 0.15 mm. Run-out at centre bearing: 0.02 mm max.

Location: Cylinder head, driven by toothed belt from crankshaft sprocket.

Inspection: Check bearing surfaces and cam lobes for wear. Measure lobe height - if worn more than 0.5 mm from specification, renew camshaft and followers together.

Testing Procedures

Compression Test

1

Run engine to normal operating temperature. Stop engine and remove all spark plugs.

2

Disconnect HT leads and lay them aside. Disconnect fuel pump relay or injector connector to prevent fuel delivery during cranking.

3

Fit compression tester to No.1 spark plug hole. Open throttle fully.

4

Crank engine for 4-6 revolutions. Record highest reading.

5

Repeat for all cylinders. Minimum pressure: 7.0 bar (new: 8-10 bar). Max difference between cylinders: 3.0 bar.

Oil Pressure Test

1

Remove oil pressure sender switch from crankcase (rear of cylinder head on 1.05/1.3L, filter bracket on 1.6/1.8L).

2

Fit suitable oil pressure gauge with adapter.

3

Run engine to normal temperature. Check pressure at 2000 rpm.

4

Pass criterion: Minimum 2.0 bar at 2000 rpm with oil at 80°C. If below, check oil grade/level first, then investigate pump wear or bearing clearances.

Critical: Cylinder Head Bolt Procedure

Tighten in three stages: 40 Nm, then 60 Nm, then tighten a further 180° (half turn). Bolts are stretch-type single-use - always renew. Use new bolts and tighten in the correct sequence (centre outward in spiral pattern). Engine must be cold during tightening.
