Electrical System

12V negative earth system. Main fuse box behind cover on right side of dashboard. Relay panel above fuse box. Battery on left side of engine compartment. Alternator standard on all models (no dynamo).

Battery

SpecificationValue
Voltage12V
Capacity (standard)45-63 Ah (varies by spec)
TypeMaintenance-free (sealed) from factory
LocationLeft side engine compartment
EarthNegative to body

Check: Terminals clean and tight, case not cracked. Check electrolyte level (if accessible) between MIN/MAX marks. Top up with distilled water only. Apply petroleum jelly to terminals after reconnecting.

Alternator Test

1

Check drivebelt tension and condition. Replace if glazed or cracked.

2

With engine running at 2000 rpm, battery voltage should be 13.5-14.5V at terminals.

3

Turn on headlights and heater fan. Voltage should remain above 13.0V. If drops below, alternator or regulator fault.

4

Check charge warning light: should illuminate with ignition on, go out when engine starts.

Starter Motor

TypeBosch pre-engaged, 12V 0.9-1.2 kW
LocationRear of engine, below inlet manifold
Mounting boltsM10, 45 Nm

Starter Testing

Solenoid click but no crank: Check battery voltage (should be 12.4V+), check main cable and earth connections, check solenoid trigger wire.
Slow crank: Battery low, poor earth connection, worn starter brushes, or engine tight.
No click: Check fuse, ignition switch, inhibitor switch (auto), clutch switch (manual).

Fuses & Relays

Fuse box behind removable cover on right side of dashboard, visible when driver's door is open. Relays located above fuse box on a panel.

Fuse Box Layout (Typical)

Slot 1: 10A Indicator, rev light
Slot 2: 10A dipped beam L
Slot 3: 10A dipped beam R
Slot 4: 10A main beam L
Slot 5: 10A main beam R
Slot 6: 10A Tail L, number plate, instruments
Slot 7: 10A Tail R, fog rear
Slot 8: 15A Horn, brake lights
Slot 9: 15A Interior light, cigar lighter, clock
Slot 10: 15A Washer/wiper, rear wiper
Slot 11: 15A Heated rear window, heated mirrors
Slot 12: 15A Fresh air blower
Slot 13: spare
Slot 14-17: various options

*Varies by year and options. Always check your vehicle's fuse card.

⚡ Relay Addition Recommendations

The VW Golf MK2 factory wiring routes high current directly through dashboard switches. This causes contact arcing, melting, and fire risk. Adding relays extends switch life and improves reliability.

Headlight Main Beam Relay (Recommended for ALL models)

Problem: Main beam current (approx 10A per lamp) flows through the steering column combination switch. Contacts arc and overheat, melting the switch and potentially causing fire.

Solution: Add relay so switch carries only relay coil current (approx 150mA).

Relay: Bosch 0 332 019 150 (30A) or equivalent 4-pin
Fuse: 15A in-line on new power feed
Wire: 2.5 mm² for power feed and load

Pin 30: Fused +12V direct from battery (2.5 mm² red wire + 15A fuse)
Pin 87: To main beam bulbs (existing wire from combination switch output)
Pin 85: Chassis earth (1.0 mm² brown wire)
Pin 86: From combination switch main beam output (existing blue/white wire)

Mount relay near radiator support or battery. The combination switch now energises relay coil only. All headlight current flows via relay contacts direct from battery.

Result: Brighter headlights (full battery voltage at lamps), switch contacts protected, reduced fire risk.

Rear Heated Screen Relay

Problem: Rear screen heater draws 15-20A. Factory wiring uses dashboard switch rated for only 10A continuous. Switch contacts overheat.

Solution: Add 30A relay. Wire gauge: 4.0 mm² for heater feed (high current).

Pin 30: Fused +12V from battery (4.0 mm² + 25A fuse)
Pin 87: To rear screen heater element
Pin 85: Chassis earth
Pin 86: From existing heated screen switch output
Fan Blower Motor Relay (Optional)

Problem: Heater blower on highest speed draws 15A+. Speed resistor gets very hot. If resistor fails open, full current can bypass through switch.

Solution: If blower speed resistor has failed more than once, add relay for max speed circuit.

Pin 30: Fused +12V (4.0 mm² + 20A fuse)
Pin 87: Direct to blower motor (bypass resistor for max speed)
Pin 85: Chassis earth
Pin 86: From speed switch "max" position

Electrical Testing

Continuity Test

Use multimeter on ohms/beep range. Disconnect battery first when testing continuity. Zero reading or beep = good connection. Infinite reading = open circuit.

Voltage Drop Test

Set multimeter to DC volts. Connect between battery negative and component earth point. With component operating, reading should be less than 0.5V. Higher reading indicates poor earth connection.

Current Draw Test

Set multimeter to DC amps (10A range). Connect in series with battery negative cable. With everything off and doors closed: should be less than 50mA (parasitic drain). If higher, remove fuses one at a time to find offending circuit.

Electrical Quick Diagnosis

Total electrical failure: Check battery connections, main fuse, battery earth strap to body/chassis.
One circuit dead: Check fuse (visual + meter), check relay, check earth point for that circuit.
Dim lights: Poor earth, low battery, high resistance in old wiring, alternator fault.
Intermittent faults: Look for corroded multi-pin connectors, chafed wires at hinges (doors, tailgate), loose fuse contacts.

Source: Haynes Manual 1081, Chapters 5D & 12. *Relay recommendations supplemented from automotive electrical best practices and known VW failure data
