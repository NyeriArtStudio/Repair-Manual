Transmission

Front-wheel drive with transversely mounted engine. Transmission attached to left side of engine. Several gearbox types fitted depending on engine and year.

Clutch

SpecificationValue
TypeSingle dry plate, diaphragm spring
Pedal free play15-20 mm
Cable typeSelf-adjusting (most models)
Clutch bolts torque25 Nm (18 lbf ft)
Release bearingCarbon type, non-serviceable

Clutch Check

Slip test: Drive in top gear at 30 mph. Accelerate hard. If engine revs rise without corresponding speed increase, clutch is slipping. Causes: worn friction material, oil contamination (rear crank seal leak), cable maladjusted.
Drag test: With engine warm and running, depress clutch, select reverse. If gears crunch, clutch is dragging. Causes: cable maladjusted, warped disc, worn release bearing.

Gearbox Oil Check (084/085)

1

Vehicle on level ground. Oil must be checked cold (before driving) or at least 5 minutes after stopping.

2

Clean area around filler/level plug (on front face of gearbox). Remove plug.

3

Oil should be level with bottom edge of plug hole. If not, top up with specified oil until it trickles out. Wait for level to stabilise.

4

Refit plug with new washer. Torque: 25 Nm.

Automatic Transmission

3-speed torque converter automatic (type 090/010). Separate final drive unit.

SpecificationValue
Type3-speed, torque converter
Total capacity6.0L (dry fill)
Service drain & refill3.0L
Fluid typeDexron II or III ATF
Final drive capacity0.75L SAE 90EP
Fluid checkEngine warm, idling, selector in N

Important: Never tow automatic models with driven wheels on ground (unless at very low speed for short distance with selector in N). Towing with engine off damages transmission due to lack of lubrication.

CV Joint Check

With vehicle raised and in gear, attempt to rotate each wheel while holding driveshaft. Any appreciable movement indicates CV wear. Also check boots for cracks by turning steering to full lock and slowly rotating wheels.
