Body & Interior

Steel monocoque body with bolt-on front wings, bolt-on bonnet and tailgate. Interior trim clips into place.

Body Panel Notes

Front wings: Bolt-on, not welded. Three bolts at top (visible with bonnet open), one inside door aperture, two underneath. Remove side indicator and wheel arch liner for access.
Bonnet: Four bolts at hinges. Mark position before removal for alignment on refitting.
Doors: Bolt-on hinges with splined adjusters for alignment. Check hinge pins for wear (door drop).
Tailgate: Strut-supported. Renew struts if tailgate won't stay open (common failure).

Doors & Glass

Window Regulator

Manual or electric. Manual type uses scissor mechanism with cable and winder handle. Common failure: plastic cable guides break, causing window to drop. Regulator is riveted to door - drill out rivets, fit with bolts/nuts on reassembly.

Central Locking (where fitted)

Vacuum-operated system using pump in boot. Common faults: split vacuum lines (especially where they pass through door bellows), failed door actuators. Test: listen for pump running in boot when locking. If pump runs continuously, there's a vacuum leak.

Interior Trim

Most trim panels are clipped into place. Use plastic trim removal tools to avoid breaking clips. Common clip locations: bottom edge, sides, and sometimes hidden screws behind pull handles.

Sunroof

Manual tilting/sliding sunroof on most models. Cable-operated mechanism. Common faults: seized cables (lubricate with specified grease), broken cable drums (expensive repair), blocked drain tubes (water leaks into interior - clear with compressed air).

Drain tubes: Four tubes (one at each corner) run down A-pillars and C-pillars, exiting under body. If blocked, water overflows into headlining and footwells.

Corrosion Points

Common rust areas on Golf MK2 (check at MOT):

  • Front inner wings (behind plastic arch liners)
  • Sill seams (outer/inner joint, both sides)
  • Rear wheel arches (inner and outer)
  • Tailgate lower edge and hinge area
  • Front door bottoms (drain holes block, water sits)
  • Battery tray and surrounding area (acid damage)
  • Windscreen scuttle drains (blockage causes inner wing rot)
Bodywork Tips

Always ensure door and sunroof drain holes are clear. The factory applied wax-based underbody protection - inspect annually and repair damage with underseal spray. Do not use filler on structural repairs - replace panels where possible.

Source: Haynes Manual 1081, Chapters 11 & 12.
