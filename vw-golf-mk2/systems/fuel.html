Fuel System

The VW Golf MK2 was fitted with multiple fuel system types depending on engine, year, and market. Correct identification is essential before ordering parts or performing diagnostics.

Fuel System Safety

Fuel is highly flammable. Always disconnect the battery earth lead before working on the fuel system. Work in a well-ventilated area. Do not smoke or use naked flames. Relieve fuel pressure before disconnecting any lines.

Carburettor Idle Adjustment Procedure

1

Run engine to normal temperature. Connect tachometer and CO meter.

2

Check ignition timing is correct (see Ignition System).

3

Adjust idle speed screw to specified rpm.

4

Adjust mixture screw (CO) to achieve specified CO content. Turning clockwise weakens mixture.

5

Recheck idle speed after CO adjustment - readjust if necessary.

K-Jetronic Fuel Injection (CIS)

Continuous Injection System - mechanically controlled, fuel pressure-dependent injection. No electronic control unit (except Lambda regulation on catalytic converter models). Used on 1.8L 8V early and all 16V engines.

Fuel Distributor

Mounted on top of the airflow sensor. Distributes fuel to injectors via individual control plunger valves. The plunger position is controlled by airflow sensor plate position.

Location: Left side of engine bay, air cleaner housing mounted on top.
System pressure: 5.0-5.4 bar
Differential pressure: Maintained constant by pressure regulator (0.4-0.8 bar above intake manifold pressure)

Testing: Connect fuel pressure gauge to test port. Cranking pressure should be 4.5-5.5 bar. If low, suspect fuel pump, filter, or pressure regulator.

Warm-Up Regulator (WUR)

Controls control pressure (which enriches mixture when cold by allowing more fuel through the plunger). Heated by engine coolant and electrically.

Location: Bolted to inlet manifold, coolant hoses connected.
Test: Control pressure cold: 1.1-2.1 bar. Control pressure warm: 2.9-4.2 bar. If out of spec, renew WUR.

KE-Jetronic (16V only)

Electronically enhanced K-Jetronic with Electro-Hydraulic Actuator (EHA) on the fuel distributor. The EHA varies differential pressure based on Lambda sensor signal, coolant temp, and throttle position.

EHA Test: With ignition on, engine off, EHA current should be approximately 0-10 mA. At idle warm: 0 +/- 2 mA. If current is pegged at +10 or -10 mA, system is at correction limit - check Lambda sensor and air leaks.

Digijet Fuel Injection

Electronic fuel injection system used on mid-production 1.8L 8V engines. Throttle body injection with two injectors firing alternately. Replaced by Digifant in 1987.

Fuel pressure2.5-3.0 bar (regulated at pump)
Idle speed (pre-July 1989)750-850 rpm
Idle speed (July 1989+)880-980 rpm
CO content0.3-1.1% (pre-July 1989), 0.3-1.5% (July 1989+)

Digifant Fuel Injection

Bosch Digifant - fully electronic multi-point fuel injection with hot-film air mass sensor. Used on late 1.8L 8V engines. Separate ECU mounted behind glovebox or under passenger seat.

Idle speed750-850 rpm (not adjustable)
Fuel pressure2.5 bar (regulated at fuel rail)
CO content0.5-1.5%
Ignition timing5-7° BTDC at 2000-2500 rpm, temp sender disconnected

Mono-Jetronic

Single-point electronic fuel injection used on some markets. Single throttle body injector. Idle speed and mixture controlled by ECU - not adjustable.

Idle speed750-950 rpm (not adjustable)
CO content0.2-1.2%

Fuel Pump & Delivery

Electric Fuel Pump

Electric pump located under the vehicle near the fuel tank (injected models). Carburettor models use a mechanical pump on the engine.

Fuel injected system pressure2.5-5.5 bar (varies by system)
Delivery rateMinimum 1.0 L/min
Electrical12V, 3-5A running current

Test: Disconnect fuel line at fuel distributor/rail. Connect hose to container. Jumper fuel pump relay terminals 30 and 87 (bypassing ECU). Pump should deliver 1.0 L/min minimum. If noisy or weak, renew.

OEM: Bosch 0 580 464 070
Aftermarket: Delphi FE0484, VDO 228-225-007-001Z

Fuel Pressure Testing

1

Relieve fuel pressure: remove fuel pump fuse/relay and crank engine until it stalls.

2

Connect fuel pressure gauge to test port or Schrader valve on fuel rail.

3

Reconnect pump fuse. Turn ignition on (do not start). Read static pressure.

4

Start engine. At idle, pressure should be at system specification for your fuel system type.

5

Disconnect vacuum hose from pressure regulator (if fitted). Pressure should rise by 0.3-0.5 bar.

Fuel System Quick Diagnosis

No start (injected): Check pump operation (listen at tank when ignition on), check fuel pressure, check injectors clicking (stethoscope), check fuel pump relay and fuse.
Hard start when cold: Check warm-up regulator (K-Jet), coolant temp sensor (Digifant/Digijet), choke (carb).
Running rich: Check CO setting (carb), warm-up regulator stuck cold (K-Jet), Lambda sensor (Digifant), air filter clogged.
Running lean: Check for air leaks, fuel filter, fuel pressure low, injectors dirty.
