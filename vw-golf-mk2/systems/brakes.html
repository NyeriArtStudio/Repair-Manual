Braking System

Dual-circuit hydraulic braking system with diagonally-split circuits (front left + rear right / front right + rear left). Front disc brakes on all models. Rear brakes: drums on 1.05/1.3L, discs on 1.6/1.8L. Servo-assisted on all except 1.05L base models.

Front Disc Brakes

Single-piston floating caliper design. Caliper slides on guide pins which must be kept lubricated with high-temperature brake grease.

Front Brake Pads

OEM: VW 191 698 151 (1.05/1.3), VW 191 698 151B (1.6/1.8)
Aftermarket: ATE 13.0460-7118.2, Bosch 0986494145, Ferodo FDB105
Check interval: Every 10,000 miles. View through wheel rim inspection hole.

Replacement: Remove lower caliper guide pin bolt. Pivot caliper upward. Remove pads and anti-rattle springs. Compress piston with G-clamp. Fit new pads. Lubricate guide pins with high-temp grease. Torque guide pin: 25-30 Nm.

Disc Runout Check

1

Remove wheel. Mount dial gauge against disc face 10mm from outer edge.

2

Rotate disc one full turn. Maximum runout: 0.10 mm. If exceeded, disc needs skimming or replacement.

3

Check disc thickness with micrometer at 8 points around disc. Must be above minimum thickness.

Rear Brakes

Rear Disc (1.6 & 1.8L models)

Integral drum handbrake inside disc hub. Separate pads for service brake (hydraulic) and shoes for handbrake (cable).

Handbrake shoe clearance: Adjust via star wheel accessible through hole in disc/hub until wheel locks, then back off 5-6 clicks.

Rear Drum (1.05 & 1.3L models)

Twin-leading shoe design. Self-adjusting mechanism on one shoe only. Common failure: self-adjuster seizes, causing long handbrake lever travel.

Drum removal: May require two M8x1.25 bolts threaded into tapped holes in drum face to press drum off hub.

Hydraulic System

Dual diagonal circuits. If one circuit fails, the other provides braking to one front and the opposite rear wheel. Warning light on dash illuminates if pressure imbalance detected.

Brake Fluid Warning

Brake fluid is hygroscopic (absorbs moisture from air). Old fluid causes corrosion in cylinders and lowers boiling point. Must be renewed every 2 years regardless of mileage. Only use fresh DOT 4 fluid from a sealed container.

Brake Pressure Regulator

Located on rear axle line. Reduces rear brake pressure during heavy braking to prevent rear wheel lockup. Some models have load-sensing type connected to rear suspension.

Test: VW recommend testing at dealer with pressure gauges. Rough test: on wet road, hard braking from 30 mph - rear wheels should not lock before fronts.

Handbrake (Parking Brake)

Mechanical cable system. Self-adjusting on rear disc models (via service brake shoe wear adjuster). Manual adjustment on drum models.

Adjustment (drum models): Remove rubber plugs from backing plate. Rotate star wheel with screwdriver until wheel locks, then back off 5-6 notches. Repeat both sides. Lever travel: 4-6 clicks.

Bleeding Procedure

1

Top up reservoir with fresh DOT 4 fluid. Keep topped up during bleeding.

2

Start with rear right (farthest from master cylinder), then rear left, front right, front left.

3

Attach bleed hose to nipple. Submerge end in clean fluid in jar.

4

Assistant pumps pedal 3 times and holds down. Open bleed nipple 1/2 turn. Fluid flows out. Close nipple before pedal reaches floor.

5

Repeat until clear bubble-free fluid emerges. Move to next wheel.

Brake Testing

Master Cylinder Check

Pedal should be firm, not spongy. If pedal slowly sinks under constant pressure, master cylinder internal seals are failing - renew.

Servo Check (where fitted)

With engine off, pump pedal until hard. Hold pressure, start engine. Pedal should drop slightly as servo assistance kicks in. If no change, check servo vacuum hose and non-return valve.
