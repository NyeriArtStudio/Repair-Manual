Ignition System

Three distinct ignition systems were used on the Golf MK2: contact breaker points (early), transistorised TCI-H (mid), and fully electronic FEI (late 16V). All engines fire 1-3-4-2 with No.1 at the crankshaft pulley end.

High Voltage Warning

Ignition HT voltage can be dangerous, especially to people with heart problems or pacemakers. Do not work on the ignition system with the engine running or ignition switched on.

Points Adjustment Procedure

1

Remove distributor cap and rotor arm. Clean contact surfaces with solvent.

2

Turn engine until points are fully open (heel on cam peak).

3

Check gap with feeler gauge: 0.4 mm. Adjust by loosening fixed contact screw and repositioning.

4

Check dwell angle with dwell meter while cranking: 44-50°. Increase gap to reduce dwell, decrease gap to increase dwell.

5

Recheck ignition timing with stroboscopic light after any points adjustment.

No contact breaker points. A reluctor sensor in the distributor triggers the transistorised amplifier. More reliable and consistent than points.

Important: Coil-Spark Plug Matching

Must Match Coil and Plugs

Some models have a modified ignition coil identified by a grey sticker (instead of green). This modified coil must be used with single-electrode spark plugs (N9YCC 0.8 mm gap). Do not mix new plugs with old coils or vice versa.

Fully Electronic Ignition (FEI) - 16V Only

Used on the 1.8L 16V GTi (RP). Hall effect sensor in distributor, vacuum advance/retard unit. Integrated ignition amplifier.

Key Components

Ignition Coil

Located on the left side of the engine bay (when facing front). Green sticker = original type (use with 0.7/0.8 mm gap plugs). Grey sticker = modified type (use with 0.8 mm single-electrode plugs only).

Primary resistance: 0.5-0.8 Ω
Secondary resistance: 3.0-4.0 kΩ

Test: Disconnect LT leads. Measure primary between + and - terminals. Measure secondary between + terminal and HT tower. Renew if out of spec.

OEM: Bosch 0 221 122 025
Aftermarket: Delphi CE20009, NGK U1010

Distributor

Driven from the end of the camshaft. Contains either contact breaker points (early), reluctor sensor (TCI-H), or Hall sensor (FEI).

Test - TCI-H reluctor: Disconnect 2-pin sensor connector. Measure resistance across sensor terminals: 0.8-1.2 kΩ. If open circuit or short, renew distributor.

Test - FEI Hall sensor: With ignition on, check voltage at Hall connector: 0V and 5V (supply). With engine cranking, signal should pulse 0-5V.

Testing Procedures

Ignition Timing (Stroboscopic Method)

1

Run engine to normal operating temperature. Connect timing light and tachometer.

2

On carb and K-Jet models: disconnect and plug distributor vacuum hose. On Digifant: disconnect temp sender.

3

Run at idle speed. Point timing light at crankshaft pulley marks. Check alignment with specification for your engine.

4

If adjustment needed: loosen distributor clamp bolt. Rotate distributor body: clockwise retards timing, anti-clockwise advances timing.

5

Gradually increase engine speed while observing timing mark. Should move opposite to rotation direction (proving centrifugal advance is working). If not, distributor weights are seized - renew distributor.

Ignition Quick Diagnosis

No spark at all cylinders: Check coil, HT lead from coil to distributor, distributor cap, rotor arm, ignition amplifier fuse.
No spark one cylinder: Swap HT lead/plug with known good cylinder. If follows, plug/lead fault. If stays, distributor cap crack or rotor fault.
Weak spark: Check coil resistance, condenser (points type), battery voltage (should be 12.4V+), earth connections.
Intermittent misfire: Check HT leads for tracking (dark lines on insulation), coil tower for corrosion, distributor cap for moisture.
