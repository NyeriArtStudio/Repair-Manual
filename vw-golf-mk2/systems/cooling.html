Cooling & HVAC System

Pressurised cooling system with expansion tank, belt-driven coolant pump, and thermostatically-controlled cooling fan. Capacity: 6.3 litres (50% antifreeze/water mix minimum).

Scalding Warning

Never remove the radiator or expansion tank cap when the engine is hot. The system is pressurised and hot coolant can cause severe scalding. Wait until engine is cold.

Radiator Replacement

1

Drain cooling system by opening radiator drain plug and expansion tank cap.

2

Disconnect upper and lower radiator hoses and expansion tank hose.

3

On AC models: disconnect refrigerant lines from condenser (requires professional evacuation).

4

Remove mounting bolts and lift radiator assembly upwards.

5

Refit in reverse order. Renew all hose clips. Refill with correct antifreeze mixture. Bleed system.

Thermostat

TypeWax element, bypass type
Opening temperature80-84°C (standard), 87-92°C (hot climate optional)
Fully open temperature95°C
LocationHousing on front of cylinder head, upper radiator hose connection

Test Procedure

1

Remove thermostat from housing. Note orientation of jiggle pin (must face upward on refitting).

2

Suspend thermostat in container of water with thermometer. Heat water gradually.

3

Check opening temperature: should begin opening at 80-84°C. If not, renew.

4

At 95°C, valve should be fully open with minimum lift of 8 mm.

Water Pump

Centrifugal pump mounted on front of engine block. Also serves as timing belt tensioner on early models (by rotating on eccentric mount). Driven by toothed belt.

TypeCentrifugal, belt-driven
Tensioner function (pre-1985)Eccentric mount - rotating adjusts belt tension
Seal typeCeramic face seal (non-serviceable)

Failure signs: Coolant leak from weep hole (indicates seal failure), bearing noise, excessive timing belt vibration.

Note: Water pump seal is not separately available. If leaking, renew complete pump.

Cooling Fan

Electric radiator fan controlled by thermostatic switch in radiator or cylinder head. Two-speed operation on most models.

Switch LocationOn TemperatureOff Temperature
Radiator (standard)92-97°C84-91°C
Cylinder head (some models)99-105°C90-98°C

Thermostatic Fan Switch

Location: Lower radiator tank or rear of cylinder head (varies by model).
Test: Remove switch, suspend in water with thermometer. Using multimeter on continuity: should close circuit (beep) at switch-on temperature and open at switch-off temperature.
OEM: VW 251 959 481 / FEBI 17695

Cooling System Quick Diagnosis

Overheating: Check coolant level, thermostat stuck closed, fan not running (check fuse, relay, switch), water pump failure, radiator blocked externally or internally, head gasket leak (check for oil in coolant or combustion gases).
Running too cool: Thermostat stuck open, fan running continuously (faulty switch or shorted wiring).
Coolant loss: External leak (hoses, radiator, pump weep hole, heater matrix), internal leak (head gasket into cylinder - check for white smoke, or into oil - check for mayo on dipstick).

Heater System

Water valve on bulkhead controls coolant flow to heater matrix. Cable-operated temperature control, electrically-operated fan with 4 speeds. Common failure: heater matrix leaking (requires dashboard removal to replace).

Heater Matrix Replacement Tips

This is a major job requiring partial dashboard removal. If matrix is leaking, check heater pipe unions first (external connections at bulkhead) - these often corrode and can be replaced much more easily.

Air Conditioning

Optional on most models. Compressor driven by separate V-belt from crankshaft. Refrigerant: R12 (early) or R134a (retrofitted models).

Compressor typeSanden SD709 or VW/Zexel
Drivebelt tension5-10 mm deflection on longest run
Refrigerant (original)R12 (CFC - now illegal to vent)
Retrofit refrigerantR134a (requires POE oil and new seals)

Warning: Air conditioning refrigerant forms a poisonous gas if exposed to flame. Work must be carried out by qualified technicians with proper recovery equipment.

Cooling System Pressure Test

1

Attach cooling system pressure tester to expansion tank in place of cap.

2

Pump pressure to 1.5 bar. Hold for 5 minutes. Pressure should not drop more than 0.1 bar.

3

If pressure drops: check all hose connections, radiator, pump, heater unions for external leaks.

4

If no external leak: suspect head gasket. Remove spark plugs and pressurise - check for coolant in cylinders.

Source: Haynes Manual 1081, Chapter 3.
