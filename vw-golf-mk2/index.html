Volkswagen Golf MK2 & Jetta

1984 - 1992 (excl. Convertible, Rallye, Caddy, Diesel, 4WD, and post-Feb 1992 models)

Vehicle Overview

The Mk. II Volkswagen Golf and Jetta range was introduced in March 1984, with revised body and trim features being the main visual difference to the earlier range. The engine/transmission is mounted transversely at the front of the vehicle, drive being to the front wheels. Detailed improvements were made throughout the years to improve power output and economy.

ℹ Key Production Breakpoint

August 1985 is the critical production split. Pre-August 1985 engines use mechanical rocker fingers with adjustable valve clearances. Post-August 1985 engines use hydraulic bucket tappets (self-adjusting). Always identify your engine before ordering parts or performing valve work.

⚠ Timing Belt Critical Warning

The timing belt must be renewed every 40,000 miles (60,000 km). This is an interference engine - belt failure will cause piston-to-valve contact and catastrophic engine damage. Always renew the tensioner and idler roller at the same time.
